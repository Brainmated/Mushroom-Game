SECTION 01

Player Movement & Combat

Movement

style8-directional top-down. Diagonal velocity normalized — no speed bonus on diagonals.
inputWASD movement. Mouse aim for all weapons. Space for dodge roll. Gamepad supported.
feelInstant acceleration with a very short lerp. No physics simulation — snappy and responsive.
speedBase 200 px/s. Items add flat bonuses via move_speed stat.
room transitionPlayer must be moving toward a door for it to trigger. Velocity is zeroed on spawn in the new room — prevents chain transitions.

Shovel — pure utility tool big twist anchor

identityThe shovel does NOT deal damage. It is a utility tool only. One use per room — dig or swing, the action is consumed either way.
digDigDetector RayCast2D checks for diggable tiles (physics layer 4) in the aim direction. If can_dig is true and a tile is found, a dig is performed — emitting dig_used, dig_attempted and tile_dug signals.
swing fallbackIf no diggable tile is present or can_dig is false, the shovel plays a short visible flash swing. No damage dealt.
cooldown0.5s on both dig and swing. Modified by attack_speed_multiplier.
resetcan_dig resets to true every time the player enters a new room via reset_dig().
The shovel opens the underground layer — the game's defining mechanic. Design space is intentionally reserved here for a later phase.

Sword — primary weapon

visualAlways visible sprite rotating to face the mouse cursor via WeaponPivot Node2D.
attack120° arc tweened with EASE_OUT. Starts −60° from aim, ends +60°. Hitbox (CircleShape2D) active the entire sweep. Left mouse button.
cooldown0.6s base, divided by attack_speed_multiplier.
rangeHitbox circle radius — base 35px. Increased by sword_range stat from items. Visibly larger arc.
dodge rollSpace. Short dash in movement direction (or aim direction if standing still). Grants i-frames for dodge duration. 1.0s cooldown.
i-framesDodge roll only — not on swings. 0.8s i-frames on hit received, separate from dodge.
Sword = damage Shovel = utility only Dodge = survival

SECTION 02

Damage, Health & Player Stats

Health system

base HP6 hearts (12 half-hearts). Displayed as heart icons in the HUD.
damage takenEnemies deal half-heart increments. Boss attacks deal full hearts. Armour absorbs flat damage before HP loss.
healingFound in idle rooms, dropped by mini-bosses. No passive regeneration.
heart containersMax HP raised or lowered by items. Negative items cannot reduce max HP below 2 hearts (1 full heart). Current HP clamped to new max on pickup.
hit i-frames0.8s invincibility after taking damage. Visual sprite flash as indicator.

Shovel damage has been removed entirely. The shovel is a pure utility tool and will never deal damage under any circumstances.

Death & roguelite loop

on deathRun ends. Permadeath. Return to main menu / hub. No mid-run saves.
meta-progressionPersistent unlocks: new items enter pool, new room variants, cosmetics. later
run seedEvery run has a visible seed. Displayed on death screen for sharing and reproducibility. Also useful for debugging specific runs.

SECTION 03

Room Types & Layouts

Room catalogue

🏠 Starting room built
No enemies. All 4 doors open. Player spawns at center. West door leads to treasure room. All other doors lead to combat rooms.
⚔️ Combat room built
Doors lock on entry. Enemies placed procedurally by RoomGenerator into spawn zone. All enemies must be defeated to unlock doors.
💤 Idle room
No enemies. Trinkets or consumables on the floor. Environmental detail. No combat.
💎 Treasure room built
2 mushroom items on hovering pedestals. Walk over one to collect it — the other vanishes. Stat changes applied via ItemData.apply().
🔒 Secret room
No door marker. Found by digging a specific thin wall tile with the shovel. Rare item inside.
👹 Mini-boss room
One strong enemy. Guaranteed heart container or mid-tier item on defeat.
💀 Boss room
One per directional zone. Multi-phase boss fight. Guaranteed major item drop. Defeating it unlocks the next zone or ends the run.
Secret rooms use the shovel — not explosives like BoI. The player digs a diggable thin-wall tile to reveal the hidden entrance. Thematic, distinctive, and mechanically unique.

Layout philosophy

room size1920×1080px. All rooms are the same dimensions — keeps door alignment consistent.
door detectionVelocity-based. Player must be moving toward a door (velocity threshold ±10 px/s in door's axis) to trigger a transition.
spawn pointsEach room has 4 Marker2D spawn points (SpawnNorth, SpawnSouth, SpawnEast, SpawnWest) placed 100px+ inward from each door.
templatesHand-crafted tilemap templates as PackedScenes. RoomGenerator instantiates and scatters content on top.
diggable tilestwist Tiles on physics layer 4 are diggable. DigDetector raycast checks this layer. Secret rooms hidden behind such tiles.

SECTION 04

Procedural Map Generation

Directional zone world

structureNo floor descent. One sprawling 2D room grid. Zones defined by cardinal direction from start: North, East, South, West.
zone themesEach direction has a unique biome — tileset, enemy roster, ambient tone, and boss.
zone depthManhattan distance from start. Near-centre rooms feel neutral. Deep rooms feel dangerous and themed.
boss placementBoss room at maximum depth in each direction.

Current implementation

RoomDataGodot Resource. Defines template scene, enemy pool, min/max enemy count, spawn zone Rect2, obstacle pool.
RoomGeneratorAutoload. Instantiates template, sets active_doors, creates EnemySpawnPoints node with randomly scattered Marker2Ds, returns ready room Node2D.
RoomManagerAutoload. Picks from ROOM_POOLS (generated) or DIRECT_SCENES (hand-crafted). Swaps rooms as children of World. Player is reparented not destroyed.

Full world generation later

  1. 1Place starting room at (0, 0).
  2. 2BFS expansion outward up to max-depth per zone.
  3. 3Mandatory rooms placed first (combat quota, 1 treasure, 1 mini-boss, 1 boss per zone).
  4. 4Secret rooms adjacent to dead-ends only. No door marker.
  5. 5Connect rooms by activating correct doors on adjacent pairs.
  6. 6Assign templates by zone pool, room type and depth.
  7. 7Validate reachability and mandatory counts. Re-seed on failure.
Zone gate conditions planned post-MVP: e.g. South zone requires East boss cleared first. Non-linear but structured progression without hard floor ordering.

Minimap HUD next milestone

displayCorner minimap. Rooms as small squares. Current room highlighted. Unvisited rooms dark.
iconsRoom-type icon on visited special rooms.
secret roomsNever shown until physically entered.

SECTION 05

Item Acquisition & Economy

Theme — all items are mushrooms

Every item in the game is a real mushroom or mushroom-related organism. Stat trade-offs, lore, and visual design are grounded in the mushroom's real-world properties — toxicity, rarity, colour, and ecological role.

Item categories

Passive — permanent stat modifiers Active — cooldown abilities (1 slot) Trinkets — minor bonuses (2 slots) Consumables — single-use pickups

Stat modifiers on ItemData

sword_damage_bonusFlat addition to sword_damage.
move_speed_bonusFlat addition to move_speed in px/s.
attack_speed_bonusAdded to attack_speed_multiplier — divides all weapon cooldowns.
max_hp_bonusAdds or removes heart containers. Negative allowed — floor at 2 hearts.
luck_bonusInteger. Affects item quality rolls throughout the run.
sword_range_bonusAdded to sword_range — visibly larger sweep arc.
armour_bonusFlat damage absorbed per hit.
grants_flightBool flag. Reserved for two-layer dig system. twist

Current item pool

🟣 Violet Cortinarius Cortinarius violaceus
sword_damage +1.0 luck +2 max_hp −1.0 move_speed −20.0
More mushrooms to be added. Each should have a real-world basis — toxicity maps to trade-offs, rarity maps to power, colour maps to visual identity.

How items are found

treasure rooms2 items on hovering pedestals. Walk over one — both removed, item applied.
boss dropsGuaranteed high-quality item on boss defeat.
mini-boss dropsGuaranteed heart container or mid-tier item.
idle roomsTrinkets or consumables on ground. Walk over to collect.
secret roomsRare or out-of-pool item. Reward for digging.
shop roomsCurrency-based purchases. later

Design notes

synergiesItems listen to EventBus signals — dig events, kill events, damage events. Synergy hooks built in from day one.
limitsNo hard cap on passives. 1 active slot. 2 trinket slots.
poolsGlobal pool + per-zone pool weighted by depth. Boss rooms use separate boss-tier pool.
The shovel is the natural item synergy anchor. Items that modify digging, change what is buried, or react to dig events will feel native and thematic to this game's identity.
